Investigating Algorithmic Biases: Establishing a Credible Position.

Why this matters for Equi

Algorithmic bias on social media platforms — particularly the disproportionate reach of right-wing and anti-Muslim content — is a live and pressing issue that Equi has been invited to address.

Doing so with credibility requires a clear, structured, repeatable and well-governed methodology that helps establish Equi's preferred position in key discussions.

Approach & Outputs

Investigating bias within social media algorithms starts with the simple-sounding but difficult task of defining specific questions that require structured answers to give the evidence base needed.

This requires precision, governance and a clear understanding of what is possible - which we will manage using a method called the 'Value Pyramid'. Completing this step provides the structure for the clear, defensible approach that will be adopted to investigate algorithmic bias. The evidence base that follows needs to withstand the highest levels of scrutiny and help position Equi exactly where it wants to be to lead on specific elements of this issue.

Understanding the Sources of Bias: Three Distinct Factors.

The starting point to understand bias is to distinguish between three distinct factors that can give right-wing or anti-Muslim content greater reach on social media platforms:

1. Inauthentic Coordinated Behaviour

Organised networks deliberately amplifying content — bots, coordinated inauthentic accounts, manipulation campaigns.

2. Bias Patterns

Platform architecture that structurally favours certain content types, emotional registers, or ideological framings.

3. Content Engagement Patterns

Legitimate user behaviour where certain content generates more interaction and is therefore surfaced more widely.
